## Product UI behavior

- Keep public UI user-facing. Do not expose internal implementation notes, PRD references, demo caveats, registry caveats, or production-readiness disclaimers in customer-visible pages unless the user explicitly asks for that copy.
- Treat listed sectors such as clinic services, education, suppliers, subscriptions, products, invoices, and pay-later agreements as examples of receivable Offer types, not as the product itself.
- Marketplace filters should stay compact and decision-oriented. Prefer one clear search input plus one polished type/status control over crowded chip rows or explanatory filter panels.
