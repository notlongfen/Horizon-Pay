# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are verified businesses (clinics, agencies, freelancers, B2B suppliers, SaaS companies) seeking liquidity from receivables, verified investors looking for real-world yield opportunities, and debtors who need to acknowledge and repay structured payment obligations.

Secondary audiences include Stellar ecosystem participants, fintech developers, and financial institutions interested in PayFi infrastructure.

Users engage with HorizonPay through the marketing site to understand the value proposition, then through the application interface to create, fund, acknowledge, and repay Funding Offers.

## Product Purpose

HorizonPay is a verified receivables funding marketplace on Stellar that turns future payment obligations into present liquidity. Verified businesses create Funding Offers backed by real invoices, services, subscriptions, products, or pay-later agreements. Debtors acknowledge these obligations. Verified investors fund acknowledged Offers at transparent terms. Businesses receive immediate liquidity. Debtors repay later through structured Stellar settlement flows. Soroban smart contracts track status, settlement, and investor repayment on-chain while sensitive business data remains off-chain.

Success means: businesses unlock working capital from verified receivables, investors access transparent real-world yield, debtors experience clear repayment workflows, and the Stellar ecosystem gains a native liquidity layer for receivables.

## Brand Personality

Cold, luminous, credible. The interface embodies a glacier/galaxy aesthetic—dark, deep, and precise. It feels financial and serious without being sterile, futuristic through structure rather than decoration. The visual language combines glassmorphic surfaces with cyan/lime accents against near-black backgrounds, evoking both the vastness of space and the clarity of ice.

The personality is trust-building above all: clear, specific, conservative, and expert. It avoids hype, overpromising, and casino-like aesthetics. Every visual element should imply product mechanics—Offers, verification, repayment, and settlement—not generic fintech ornamentation.

## Anti-references

Do NOT look like or evoke:

- Casino-like DeFi platforms with neon gradients and speculative yield language
- Generic SaaS cream/beige admin dashboards
- Warm earthy fintech (orange, brown, gold)
- Purple/pink gradient crypto themes
- "Mint any debt NFT" style platforms
- AI-generated sketchy illustration styles
- Overused SaaS clichés: hero metrics templates, numbered section markers on every section, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above all headings

Specific anti-examples:
- Generic Web3 yield farming interfaces
- Traditional invoice factoring brochureware
- Generic blockchain explorer UIs
- Warm startup landing pages with cream backgrounds

## Design Principles

**Verified first** — Every surface must communicate that businesses, debtors, and investors are verified. Verification is not an afterthought; it is the foundation of trust.

**Product-specific visuals** — Decorative objects should map to actual product mechanics: Offers, invoices, KYB verification, debtor acknowledgement, investor positions, Stellar settlement, and repayment flows. Avoid abstract ornamentation.

**Cinematic, not cluttered** — The homepage establishes depth through layered backgrounds (galaxy, aurora, stellar grid, particles) and parallax, but content sections remain clean and readable. Glassmorphism is used with restraint.

**Clear over clever** — Copy prioritizes clarity and specificity. Terms like "Offer," "verified receivables," "debtor acknowledgement," and "transparent terms" are preferred over marketing buzzwords. The product explains itself.

**Depth through motion** — Scroll-driven parallax enhances the spatial feel without animating layout properties or essential content. Motion is intentional, respectful of reduced-motion preferences, and never gated behind content visibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.2 AA minimum target
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations and parallax effects
- Decorative visual layers use `aria-hidden="true"`
- Maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text against backgrounds
- Focus rings preserved on all interactive elements
- Keyboard navigation supported throughout
- Screen reader compatible: essential content is text, not only visual
- Glass surfaces maintain readable text contrast despite backdrop blur
- Mobile layouts are designed, not squeezed from desktop
